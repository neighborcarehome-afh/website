Our Philosophy

Knowing Someone's Story Changes the Way We Care

A diagnosis tells us something about a person's health. It cannot tell us who they are.

Every person we meet brings with them a lifetime of experiences — the people they have loved, families they have raised, careers they have built, traditions they cherish, challenges they have overcome, and memories that continue to shape who they are.

We know the more we begin to understand someone, the more we begin to love them. That is why learning a person's story is an essential part of our assessment process. We want to know who someone was before illness, aging, or memory loss changed what others saw first — and who they continue to be today.

Our elders have so much to teach us. When we take the time to know one another, care becomes more than a service. It becomes a relationship built on dignity, respect, and genuine human connection.

Knowing someone's story also changes the practical way we care. A familiar song can bring comfort. A lifelong routine begins to make sense. A treasured photograph can spark conversation and unlock memories. A favorite movie can bring reassurance during an anxious evening.

Understanding the life behind the diagnosis helps us provide care that is not only clinically appropriate, but deeply personal. A caregiver begins to see not simply a person who needs assistance, but a whole human being whose life has meaning, history, and something still to contribute.

We may come into one another's lives as residents, families, caregivers, and neighbors — but over time, we often find ourselves in each other's care.

Why It Matters

What Is a Nursing Assessment & Why Is It Important?

A nursing assessment is a comprehensive evaluation of a person's current health, functional abilities, cognitive and emotional needs, medical history, daily routines, and the assistance they require throughout the day and night. Rather than focusing solely on medical diagnoses, it looks at how a person is actually functioning in everyday life and the level of support needed to help them live safely, comfortably, and with dignity.

A thorough assessment helps families, healthcare providers, and prospective Adult Family Homes begin with the same understanding of a person's needs — clarifying the level of assistance required and confirming a home has the staffing, training, environment, and resources necessary to safely provide care.

The assessment may include evaluation of:

  • Medical history and diagnoses
  • Current medications, treatments & allergies
  • Mobility, transfers & fall risk
  • Nutrition, swallowing & meal assistance
  • Bathing, dressing, grooming & toileting
  • Continence, catheter & ostomy care
  • Cognition, memory & decision-making
  • Communication, vision & hearing
  • Mood, anxiety & behavioral needs
  • Sleep patterns & nighttime care
  • Pain management & comfort
  • Skin integrity
  • Nursing treatments & therapies
  • Personal routines & preferences
  • Social, emotional & spiritual needs
  • Safety concerns
For individuals seeking private-pay admission to a Washington Adult Family Home, a qualifying pre-admission nursing assessment is required before admission. The completed assessment provides the foundation for developing an individualized care plan and helps ensure a safe, thoughtful transition into the next stage of care.
How It Works

What Does the Assessment Process Look Like?

Four steps, from first questionnaire to a completed care plan.

1

Complete the Pre-Assessment Questionnaire

The prospective resident or someone who knows them well completes our secure questionnaire, sharing health history, medications, routines, preferences, and care needs. Families can upload medication lists, medical records, discharge paperwork, POLST, or Power of Attorney documents.

2

Meet & Assess

Tami meets with the prospective resident, reviews available records and medications, speaks with the family or representative, and personally evaluates current abilities and care needs — wherever the resident currently is.

3

Complete the Assessment

Information from the resident, family, medical records, and current care team is combined into a comprehensive nursing assessment identifying needs, strengths, risks, preferences, and the assistance required for safe daily care.

4

Plan the Next Step

Moving into a Neighbor Care Home? The assessment becomes the foundation of your care plan. Chosen another home? We share the completed assessment with them to help prepare for a successful admission.
